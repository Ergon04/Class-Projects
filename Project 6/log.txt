Overview
Unfortunately, there's been an escape at Dino Park! It's now your job to make sure visitors are safe by staffing research field stations across the park. You have a budget to hire researchers, and each researcher can effectively monitor different combinations of field stations. Your goal is to deploy researchers strategically to maximize monitoring coverage and keep more guests safe.

In this assignment, you will implement recursive algorithms to solve different variants of this dinosaur monitoring optimization problem:

Greedy Monitoring: Maximize the number of research stations covered with a limited number of researchers
Optimal Monitoring: Exhaustively find the best deployment to maximize station coverage with a hiring limit
Cost-Aware Monitoring: Minimize total researcher budget while meeting minimum station coverage requirements
This assignment requires careful thinking about recursive problem decomposition, algorithm design, and the trade-offs between computational efficiency and solution optimality.

Learning Objectives
After completing this assignment, you should be able to:

Formulate recursive solutions by identifying base cases, recursive cases, and subproblem decomposition
Implement recursive solutions by making recursive calls and combining results
Compare recursive formulations of related problems
Explain why recursion is a useful problem-solving tool
Develop comprehensive unit tests including manual tests and fuzz tests
Use reference implementations as validation tools
